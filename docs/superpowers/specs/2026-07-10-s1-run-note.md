## Mechanism audit (the FLAT-result discipline)

A flat sweep is not accepted as "no effect" until the knob is shown wired and the
masking mechanism named. Chain, in order:

1. **Wired?** Yes — arm headers carry the spread ({0.4, 0.6, 0.8, 1.0} at δ=0.3);
   per-path reservation churn moves with δ.
2. **Operating point?** Two one-knob exploratory excursions, both still δ-flat in
   outcomes: slack 5→0.5 s (compliance drops to 0.867 — identically at every δ);
   arrival 1→4 Hz, decoder at 80% (0.9848 at every δ).
3. **Coupling scale, measured:** request→herald latency in the calm δ=0.3 arm is
   4.17 ms mean / 10.1 ms p90, retries included — the per-attempt price is the
   0.01 s reconfig delay, three orders of magnitude under the 0.5–5 s slacks.
4. **Attempt-price excursion** (reconfig 0.01→0.5 s): outcomes finally move
   (first nonzero `late`; round.failed ~35% as every scoring retry re-pays the
   herald) but the δ-spread stays inside binomial noise at n=384, non-monotone.
5. **Model-guided binding point** (price 0.5 s + slack 2.0 s, horizon 1600 s,
   n=1580): **prediction falsified.** Predicted monotone δ-decrease ≥2 pp at
   δ=0.3; observed spread 0.95 pp ≈ exactly binomial noise (0.92 pp), non-monotone.
   The system is badly stressed (compliance 0.84, dropped 14.7%) — equally at
   every spread.

## The mechanism (code-confirmed)

The falsified prediction assumed a round pays its path's price. It doesn't:
path choice runs per lease **at every arrival, retries included**
(engine.py `_build_round_context`, the B1 seam contract), so each retry re-rolls
the path under round-robin. Rotating-blind assignment averages the per-attempt
herald probability to p̄ at first order — **the spread cancels out of the outcome
column by construction of the blind policy's fine structure, at every operating
point.** The Jensen penalty (E[1/p] > 1/p̄, +12% expected attempts at δ=0.3)
lands only where assignment is sticky per path — the background pool
replenishment — which is exactly the column that moved.

**Two-column statement:** under rotating-blind assignment, spatial heterogeneity
is invisible in outcomes and paid entirely as churn (switch reservations, pool
wastage). "Spatial blindness is free" would be the wrong reading twice over:
the cost is real but lives in the churn column, and the outcome-immunity is a
property of *rotation*, not of blindness — a sticky-blind policy (lease pinned
to a path chosen without quality information) would pay E[1/p] in the outcome
column. The policy's fine structure matters more than its awareness, at first
order.

## S2 pre-run prediction (recorded before any S2 run exists)

S2 compares `best_heralding` against `round_robin` (prereg verdict space:
DECISION-EARNED / REPRESENTATION-CHEAP, mirroring T3). From the mechanism above:

- Routing will change maximally (all leases argmax to the p=1.0 path at δ>0;
  contention-free at 0.4% utilization).
- Outcome payoff bound: (1/p̄ − 1/p_max) × attempt price ≈ 0.43 attempts × 0.01 s
  ≈ 4 ms against 5 s slack → **outcomes indifferent at the precommitted point.**
- The payoff, if visible anywhere, is the churn column: fewer reservations and
  less pool wastage than round-robin at equal δ.
- Expected verdict: REPRESENTATION-CHEAP (comparative read of the existing epoch
  table, no new state) but NOT DECISION-EARNED at this operating point — the
  decision's outcome payoff sits under the same attempt-price threshold that
  hides blindness's cost. Symmetric statement: blindness costs churn, awareness
  saves churn, outcomes indifferent — while attempt price ≪ slack.

## S2 result (run after the prediction above was committed in bd4615c)

Artifacts: runs/sweep-s2 (best_heralding over the same amended grid, T1-open
physics). All arms CONVERGED. Prediction confirmed in full:

- **Routing changes maximally:** ~99% of reservations concentrate on the argmax
  path (387/398 at δ=0.3 on the p=1.0 path; lexicographic tie-break at δ=0).
- **Outcomes indifferent:** 382/384 in deadline at every δ — bit-identical to
  round-robin.
- **Churn pays, more than predicted:** at δ=0.3, reservations 657 → 398 (−39%),
  pool.expired 111 → 4 (−96%), replenish_abandoned 706 → 398.

One unpredicted decomposition: the δ=0 arm (no quality signal — pure
concentration via tie-break) already saves churn (reservations 612 → 505,
pool.expired 85 → 1), because concentrating demand keeps one pool fresh instead
of letting four age out. So S2's churn payoff splits into a **concentration
effect** (pool anti-staleness, available to any sticky policy, quality-blind or
not) and a **quality effect** (the additional 505 → 398 at δ=0.3, from
heralding at p=1.0 instead of p̄). The perishable-pool dynamics reward
concentration independently of quality-awareness — a point the parked
perishable-good abstraction will want.

**Verdict (prereg S2 space):** REPRESENTATION-CHEAP confirmed by construction
(comparative read of the existing epoch table, no new state). DECISION-EARNED
**in the churn currency only** — the decision changes routing and buys −39%
switch reservations / −96% pool expiries — and NOT in the outcome currency at
this operating point, where the payoff bound (≈4 ms vs 5 s slack) holds as
predicted. Rigged-result warning carried verbatim: one frozen epoch means this
licenses comparative reading at fixed quality only — nothing here is evidence
about prediction over time (that is B2/T4 territory, blocked on Q7/Q8).

## CORRECTION (2026-07-10, same day, external adversarial review)

An external review (ChatGPT 5.6-Sol, solicited by Tony) traced the mechanism
claim into the implementation and found it wrong. Verified against the code
and the event record; the review is right.

**What the code actually implements — three different "retries":**
1. **Foreground herald retry is STICKY-IN-PLACE** (`_on_herald_attempt`,
   engine.py:627-633): a failed herald re-attempts the SAME path with the
   reservation retained, after `HERALD_RETRY_INTERVAL_S = 1e-4 s` — a
   hard-coded constant, not a config knob. No rotation, no reconfig cost.
2. **Pool replenishment pays per attempt** (§8.2, engine.py:755+): one
   herald attempt per trigger; each trigger re-acquires a reservation and
   re-pays `reconfig_delay_s`.
3. **Round-lineage retry rotates** (`_build_round_context`): only when a
   round FAILS and re-arrives does path choice re-roll.

**What this changes:** the "rotating-blind assignment averages per-attempt
p to p̄ by construction" mechanism is NOT implemented in the foreground.
Rounds ARE sticky within a herald loop; the Jensen penalty E[1/p] > 1/p̄ is
present in foreground heralding — it is just priced at 100 µs per extra
attempt against seconds of slack. **The outcome-flatness is scale-induced,
not structurally cancelled.** My reconfig_delay excursions raised the wrong
price: path acquisition (paid once per reservation, p-independent), not the
per-attempt cycle time (the constant nothing swept — the flat-result lesson
one level deeper: the binding knob wasn't flat, it wasn't a knob at all).

**What survives, strengthened:** the two-column result stands, and the
corrected mechanism predicts it QUANTITATIVELY. Per-path pool
attempts-per-deposit in the rr δ=0.3 run: measured 2.53/1.82/1.27/1.00 vs
1/p = 2.50/1.67/1.25/1.00 — the pool protocol is a direct Jensen meter.
Predicted S2 reservation reduction 1−1/E[1/p] ≈ 37.7%; observed 39%. The
"4.17 ms mean herald latency" measured earlier is a mixture artifact
(~60% pool withdrawals at ~0 ms, ~40% live heralds at ~10 ms reconfig).

**Corrected statement:** spatial heterogeneity is invisible in outcomes
because the per-attempt price (herald cycle time) is microseconds against
second-scale slacks; it is visible in churn because pool replenishment pays
acquisition + reconfig per attempt and per-path pools are sticky by
construction. The rotate-vs-stick distinction remains real but lives at the
lineage/protocol level, not the attempt level. The S2 pre-run prediction's
CONCLUSIONS were confirmed, but partly for the wrong reason as written.

**The experiment this licenses:** promote the herald attempt cycle time to
a config knob and sweep it. Two DIFFERENT first-order quantities (external
review round 2 flagged the conflation): the **blind-policy heterogeneity
penalty** (E[1/p] − 1/p̄) × T_attempt (≈0.176 attempts × T at δ=0.3 —
what a round-robin δ-sweep measures) vs the **awareness payoff**
(E[1/p] − 1/p_max) × T_attempt (≈0.604 attempts × T — what best-vs-blind
measures). The excursion below is round_robin-only: it tests whether
heterogeneity reaches outcomes, NOT whether awareness recovers it.

**Pre-run prediction (recorded before the knob exists in config):**
at T_attempt = 0.25 s, slack 2.0 s, otherwise T1-open physics, horizon
1600 s (n≈1580), amended grid: compliance decreases monotonically in δ.
Sticky arithmetic per worst-path round at δ=0.3: mean herald 2.5 × 0.25 =
0.625 s, P(herald alone > 2 s) = P(attempts ≥ 9) = 0.6^8 ≈ 1.7%, plus
slack-penalty scoring failures rising with herald latency. Expected
δ=0.3 vs δ=0 compliance drop ≥ 2 pp (binomial noise ≈ 0.9 pp at this n).
δ=0 anchor must stay clean; default T_attempt = 1e-4 must reproduce
existing traces byte-identically (the knob's default IS the old constant).
If this prediction fails too, the corrected mechanism model is also wrong
and the honest state is "no validated mechanism story" — record it that
way.

**Result (runs/sweep-s1-attempt-price, all arms CONVERGED): PARTIAL.**
- Qualitative claim CONFIRMED: first δ-responsive outcome in seven sweeps.
  Compliance 0.9709/0.9766/0.9677/0.9595 across δ — δ=0.3 vs δ=0 is a
  1.14 pp drop. round.failed shows the
  direct dose cleanly: 0.0797 → 0.1184 (+49% relative). The herald attempt
  cycle time is the coupling knob, as the corrected mechanism said.
- **Paired accounting (added after review round 2; the naive "2.7σ vs
  binomial noise" figure treated common-seed arms as independent):** all
  1580 round lineages pair exactly across the δ=0 and δ=0.3 arms
  (`paired_outcomes.json`, committed). Discordant counts: 36 succeed only
  at δ=0, 18 only at δ=0.3; McNemar χ² (continuity-corrected) = 5.35,
  p ≈ 0.021. The deficit is real and two-sided — heterogeneity also flips
  some rounds TOWARD success, consistent with the δ=0.1 uptick being more
  than pure noise.
- Quantitative claim FAILED: predicted ≥2 pp; observed 1.14 pp. Strict
  monotonicity also fails at δ=0.1 (+0.57 pp above anchor, ~1.3σ,
  noise-compatible; the prereg's dose-response reading rule applies).
- Attribution of the magnitude miss (post-hoc, labeled as such; CONFOUND
  added in review round 3): two contributors, not one. (i) retry_cap=4
  RECOVERS most latency-induced failures — the failure-event dose (+49%)
  is damped before reaching compliance. (ii) The prediction arithmetic was
  one interval too high: the first attempt fires immediately on
  activation (engine.py `_start_heralding` pushes at now), so mean herald
  wait is (1/p − 1)·T_retry, not (1/p)·T_retry — every lineage was
  overcharged 0.25 s against 2 s slack. The DIFFERENCE formulas are
  unaffected (the −1 cancels), so the penalty/payoff coefficients stand;
  the absolute-latency inputs to the ≥2 pp claim do not. The damping
  factor cannot be isolated until the arithmetic is redone with the
  correct absolute latencies (with the retry_cap sweep as the empirical
  check).
- Standing state: the coupling-knob identification is validated; the
  magnitude model is not yet trusted to first order. A retry_cap sweep at
  this operating point would separate the damping factor — noted as a
  future excursion.

## Physicist-facing consequence

*(Rewritten in review round 3 — the original paragraph here restated the
pre-correction rotation account and survived two correction passes because
nobody re-read the conclusion after fixing the middle. The superseded text
is in git history at c630489 and earlier.)*

The foreground control variable this battery actually tested is the
**failure-to-next-attempt spacing while retaining the configured path**
(the retain-and-retry protocol branch). Switch reconfiguration time enters
the foreground price only under the OTHER branch (release-and-reacquire),
which our model implements for pool replenishment but not for rounds —
and which of the two branches real hardware implements is exactly Q4's
protocol question. Note the knob models the SPACING only: attempts
themselves take zero simulated time; the hardware ask remains the full
failure-to-next-attempt cycle.

Bounded statement of the result (review round 3 wording, adopted): in a
low-load, frozen-epoch, single-lease regime, increasing the modeled
failure-to-next-attempt spacing moves spatial path quality from a
churn-only concern into the outcome column. At the binding tested point,
balanced blind assignment suffers from path heterogeneity while an argmax
path policy produces a large paired improvement. Two sampled spacings
(100 µs, 250 ms) bracket a transition at THIS operating point; the
boundary's location and shape remain functions of the p-distribution,
slack, retry cap, load, success model, topology, and policy. Q4 is
thereby EARNED as a hardware question — not validated as a universal
threshold.
