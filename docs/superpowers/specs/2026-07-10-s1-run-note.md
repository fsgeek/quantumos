## Mechanism audit (the FLAT-result discipline)

A flat sweep is not accepted as "no effect" until the knob is shown wired and the
masking mechanism named. Chain, in order:

1. **Wired?** Yes — arm headers carry the spread ({0.4, 0.6, 0.8, 1.0} at δ=0.3);
   per-path reservation churn moves with δ.
2. **Operating point?** Two one-knob exploratory excursions, both still δ-flat in
   outcomes: slack 5→0.5 s (compliance drops to 0.867 — identically at every δ);
   arrival 1→4 Hz, decoder at 80% (0.9848 at every δ).
3. **Coupling scale, measured:** request→herald latency in the calm δ=0.3 arm is
   4.17 ms mean / 10.1 ms p90, retries included — the per-attempt price is the
   0.01 s reconfig delay, three orders of magnitude under the 0.5–5 s slacks.
4. **Attempt-price excursion** (reconfig 0.01→0.5 s): outcomes finally move
   (first nonzero `late`; round.failed ~35% as every scoring retry re-pays the
   herald) but the δ-spread stays inside binomial noise at n=384, non-monotone.
5. **Model-guided binding point** (price 0.5 s + slack 2.0 s, horizon 1600 s,
   n=1580): **prediction falsified.** Predicted monotone δ-decrease ≥2 pp at
   δ=0.3; observed spread 0.95 pp ≈ exactly binomial noise (0.92 pp), non-monotone.
   The system is badly stressed (compliance 0.84, dropped 14.7%) — equally at
   every spread.

## The mechanism (code-confirmed)

The falsified prediction assumed a round pays its path's price. It doesn't:
path choice runs per lease **at every arrival, retries included**
(engine.py `_build_round_context`, the B1 seam contract), so each retry re-rolls
the path under round-robin. Rotating-blind assignment averages the per-attempt
herald probability to p̄ at first order — **the spread cancels out of the outcome
column by construction of the blind policy's fine structure, at every operating
point.** The Jensen penalty (E[1/p] > 1/p̄, +12% expected attempts at δ=0.3)
lands only where assignment is sticky per path — the background pool
replenishment — which is exactly the column that moved.

**Two-column statement:** under rotating-blind assignment, spatial heterogeneity
is invisible in outcomes and paid entirely as churn (switch reservations, pool
wastage). "Spatial blindness is free" would be the wrong reading twice over:
the cost is real but lives in the churn column, and the outcome-immunity is a
property of *rotation*, not of blindness — a sticky-blind policy (lease pinned
to a path chosen without quality information) would pay E[1/p] in the outcome
column. The policy's fine structure matters more than its awareness, at first
order.

- Expected verdict: REPRESENTATION-CHEAP (comparative read of the existing epoch

## CORRECTION (2026-07-10, same day, external adversarial review)

An external review (ChatGPT 5.6-Sol, solicited by Tony) traced the mechanism
claim into the implementation and found it wrong. Verified against the code
and the event record; the review is right.

**What the code actually implements — three different "retries":**
1. **Foreground herald retry is STICKY-IN-PLACE** (`_on_herald_attempt`,
   engine.py:627-633): a failed herald re-attempts the SAME path with the
   reservation retained, after `HERALD_RETRY_INTERVAL_S = 1e-4 s` — a
   hard-coded constant, not a config knob. No rotation, no reconfig cost.
2. **Pool replenishment pays per attempt** (§8.2, engine.py:755+): one
   herald attempt per trigger; each trigger re-acquires a reservation and
   re-pays `reconfig_delay_s`.
3. **Round-lineage retry rotates** (`_build_round_context`): only when a
   round FAILS and re-arrives does path choice re-roll.

**What this changes:** the "rotating-blind assignment averages per-attempt
p to p̄ by construction" mechanism is NOT implemented in the foreground.
Rounds ARE sticky within a herald loop; the Jensen penalty E[1/p] > 1/p̄ is
present in foreground heralding — it is just priced at 100 µs per extra
attempt against seconds of slack. **The outcome-flatness is scale-induced,
not structurally cancelled.** My reconfig_delay excursions raised the wrong
price: path acquisition (paid once per reservation, p-independent), not the
per-attempt cycle time (the constant nothing swept — the flat-result lesson
one level deeper: the binding knob wasn't flat, it wasn't a knob at all).

**What survives, strengthened:** the two-column result stands, and the
corrected mechanism predicts it QUANTITATIVELY. Per-path pool
attempts-per-deposit in the rr δ=0.3 run: measured 2.53/1.82/1.27/1.00 vs
1/p = 2.50/1.67/1.25/1.00 — the pool protocol is a direct Jensen meter.
Predicted S2 reservation reduction 1−1/E[1/p] ≈ 37.7%; observed 39%. The
"4.17 ms mean herald latency" measured earlier is a mixture artifact
(~60% pool withdrawals at ~0 ms, ~40% live heralds at ~10 ms reconfig).

**Corrected statement:** spatial heterogeneity is invisible in outcomes
because the per-attempt price (herald cycle time) is microseconds against
second-scale slacks; it is visible in churn because pool replenishment pays
acquisition + reconfig per attempt and per-path pools are sticky by
construction. The rotate-vs-stick distinction remains real but lives at the
lineage/protocol level, not the attempt level. The S2 pre-run prediction's
CONCLUSIONS were confirmed, but partly for the wrong reason as written.

**The experiment this licenses:** promote the herald attempt cycle time to
a config knob and sweep it. Two DIFFERENT first-order quantities (external
review round 2 flagged the conflation): the **blind-policy heterogeneity
penalty** (E[1/p] − 1/p̄) × T_attempt (≈0.176 attempts × T at δ=0.3 —
what a round-robin δ-sweep measures) vs the **awareness payoff**
(E[1/p] − 1/p_max) × T_attempt (≈0.604 attempts × T — what best-vs-blind
measures). The excursion below is round_robin-only: it tests whether
heterogeneity reaches outcomes, NOT whether awareness recovers it.

**Completed two-regime statement (the S1/S2 arc's actual deliverable):**
below the attempt-price threshold, neither heterogeneity's cost nor
awareness's payoff reaches outcomes — churn is the only currency (S1/S2 at
T_attempt = 100 µs). At a binding attempt price (0.25 s against 2 s
slack), heterogeneity costs outcomes under blind routing (36 vs 18,
p ≈ 0.021) and quality-awareness recovers it decisively and then some
(39 vs 2, p ≈ 2e-8) — the payoff coefficient (E[1/p] − 1/p_max ≈ 0.604)
exceeding the blind penalty (E[1/p] − 1/p̄ ≈ 0.176), signs and ordering as
the corrected mechanism predicts. Magnitudes remain order-of-magnitude
only (retry damping unmodeled). Q4's protocol branch — retain-and-retry
vs release-and-reconfigure, and the cycle time — is confirmed as the
hinge that decides which regime a real fabric occupies.

## Physicist-facing consequence (Q6 refinement)

The threshold that decides whether spatial quality-awareness pays in *outcomes*
is the ratio of switch reconfiguration time (the failed-attempt price) to
deadline slack — not the heterogeneity magnitude, which rotation neutralizes.
Q6 should ask for the fabric's reconfiguration time on the same footing as the
path-quality spread: a fabric that reconfigures in ~10 ms below second-scale
slacks makes quality-blind rotation outcome-equivalent to quality-aware routing,
however heterogeneous the paths; churn/wear is then the only currency in play.
